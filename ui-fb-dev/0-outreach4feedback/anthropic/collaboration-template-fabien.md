## 1. Background

Luthien Research PBC builds open-source infrastructure for deploying AI control measures — monitoring, policy enforcement, and oversight — to real-world AI coding agent environments such as Claude Code.

In November 2025, Fabien Rogers and the Luthien team discussed areas where external work could complement Anthropic's internal AI safety efforts. Fabien identified three areas where the alignment science team has more work than capacity:

1. **Training data for safety monitors** — generating labeled datasets of agent behavior that could improve monitor quality
2. **Evaluations for prosaic misalignment** — building evals for real-world failure modes like agents taking excessive initiative, ignoring user intent, or making silent mistakes
3. **UX for classifier integration** — exploring how to surface classifier signals to users in agentic workflows

---

## 2. What Luthien Brings

Since that conversation, Luthien has built:

- A policy composition system that can run classifiers (LLM-based or rule-based) on live AI agent traffic, with configurable consolidation strategies
- Full conversation logging with policy intervention labels from real AI coding agent sessions — potential raw material for monitor training data
- A catalog of prosaic failure cases observed in real user sessions (e.g., false success claims, plan abandonment, self-destructive agent behavior)
- UX patterns for integrating safety classifiers into developer workflows without disrupting productivity
