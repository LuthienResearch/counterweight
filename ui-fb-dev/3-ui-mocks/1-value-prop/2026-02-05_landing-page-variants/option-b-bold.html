The problem with AI coding agents

They're powerful. They're also unpredictable.

They don't follow instructions

You write a careful claude.md. You explain what to do and what not to touch. The agent reads it, says it understands, then ignores half of it. You end up repeating yourself every session.

Scope creep happens while you're away

You write a prompt. You hit enter. You go get coffee. You come back and Claude has refactored three files you didn't ask it to touch, added a feature outside the spec, and reorganized your imports. The diff is unreviewable.

Context dies when you switch tools

You build momentum in Claude Code, hit a billing limit, switch to Codex, and start from scratch. Your session history, your working context — gone. Every tool switch is a cold start.
