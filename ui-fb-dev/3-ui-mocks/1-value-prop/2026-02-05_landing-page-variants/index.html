The problem with AI coding agents

They don't follow instructions

You write a careful claude.md. You explain what to do and what not to touch. The agent reads it, says it understands, then ignores half of it. You end up repeating yourself every session.

Scope creep happens while you're away

You write a prompt. You hit enter. You go get coffee. You come back and Claude has refactored three files you didn't ask it to touch, added a feature outside the spec, and reorganized your imports. The diff is unreviewable.

Context dies when you switch tools

You build momentum in Claude Code, hit a billing limit, switch to Codex, and start from scratch. Your session history, your working context, your conversation — gone. Every tool switch is a cold start.

How it works

1

Write your instructions

Define your agents.md or claude.md — scope boundaries, coding standards, the stuff you already do.

2

Run your prompt, go get coffee

Point your agent at Luthien with two env vars. No SDK changes, no new tools. Your agent works as before — Luthien watches.

3

Agent scope-creeps or misbehaves

It "helpfully" refactors files outside the spec, ignores your instructions, or attempts a destructive command. Business as usual.

4

Luthien detects it, retries with guardrails

Policies catch misbehavior in real-time. Block the action, retry with corrections, branch for review, or log and let through.

5

You come back to clean code

Review the diff and policy log. See what got caught and why. Summary in your terminal, inbox, or Slack.
