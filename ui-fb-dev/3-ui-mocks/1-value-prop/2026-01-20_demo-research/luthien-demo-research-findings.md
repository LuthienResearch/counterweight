# AI Tinkerers Demo Research Findings

## Executive Summary

Based on extensive research, I found limited examples of demos that directly match Luthien's planned "Who Watches The Watchbots?" presentation approach. The planned demo recreating the Replit database incident and then showing prevention through Luthien Control appears to be relatively unique in the AI Tinkerers ecosystem.

## Key Findings

### AI Tinkerers Demo Requirements
- **Duration**: 5 minutes maximum (4 minutes demo + up to 6 minutes Q&A)
- **Format**: Live demonstration preferred, no pitch decks
- **Focus**: Technical depth, show code, explain challenges and failures
- **Audience**: Technical practitioners building with LLMs
- **Style**: "Show don't tell" - practical implementation over theory

### Luthien's Planned Demo Analysis
**Title**: "Who Watches The Watchbots?"
**Approach**:
1. Recreate Replit AI agent database deletion incident
2. Replay same scenario with Luthien Control system
3. Show prevention in real-time
4. Technical focus on LiteLLM proxy integration

**Unique Positioning**:
- Direct incident recreation (rare in demo format)
- Before/after safety comparison
- Production-ready proxy implementation
- Real-world failure prevention

### Similar Demos Found (Limited Results)

After extensive searches across YouTube, GitHub, AI Tinkerers archives, and technical documentation, very few demos directly match Luthien's approach of showing AI safety control through incident recreation.

## Analysis of Similar Content

### 1. Technical AI Safety Demos
- **Gap Identified**: Most AI safety content is theoretical or academic
- **Luthien Advantage**: Practical, incident-based demonstration
- **Recommendation**: Emphasize the "incident recreation" angle as unique value

### 2. LLM Proxy/Gateway Demos
- **Available Content**: Multiple platforms (Kong, LiteLLM, HAProxy) have demos
- **Gap Identified**: None show safety incident prevention specifically
- **Luthien Advantage**: Real-world failure case study approach

### 3. AI Tinkerers Format Analysis
- **Observed Pattern**: Most successful demos show live code/systems
- **Best Practices**: Large fonts, clear narration, technical depth
- **Recommendation**: Focus on live system demonstration rather than slides
