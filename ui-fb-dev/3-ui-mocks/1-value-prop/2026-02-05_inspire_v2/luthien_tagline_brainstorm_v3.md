# Luthien Tagline Brainstorm - Round 3 (Final)

## Key Insight from Research

The best developer tool taglines share these traits:
1. **Concrete outcome** over abstract feature
2. **Emotional resolution** of a real tension
3. **Memorability** through rhythm, contrast, or brevity
4. **Specificity** that differentiates from alternatives

For Luthien specifically:
- Target: Developers who use AI coding assistants HEAVILY (not casual users)
- Pain: "I don't know what my AI is doing" / "What if it does something bad?"
- Relief: Visibility + ability to set rules
- Differentiator: Drop-in proxy (no code changes), specifically for AI coding agents

---

## Final Candidates

### Tier 1: Strongest Options

**1. Let AI code. Stay in control.**
- Formula: Permission + Reassurance
- Why it works: Resolves the core tension (AI autonomy vs. human control)
- Rhythm: 3 beats, 3 beats (balanced)
- Risk: "Control" might feel limiting to some
- Supporting text: "See every request, enforce every rule, with a drop-in proxy for AI coding agents"

**2. See what your AI is really doing.**
- Formula: Visibility promise + implied problem
- Why it works: "Really" implies current opacity; promises transparency
- Rhythm: Natural, conversational
- Risk: Might sound suspicious/paranoid
- Supporting text: "Real-time visibility and policy enforcement for Claude Code, Codex, and any AI coding agent"

**3. AI coding with guardrails.**
- Formula: Capability + Safety
- Why it works: "Guardrails" is concrete metaphor, not restrictive like "control"
- Rhythm: Simple, 4 words
- Risk: "Guardrails" becoming overused in AI discourse
- Supporting text: "Drop-in proxy to monitor, enforce rules, and trust your AI coding agent"

### Tier 2: Strong Alternatives

**4. Production-ready AI agent control.**
- Formula: Infrastructure + Capability
- Why it works: "Production-ready" signals maturity; appeals to professional devs
- Risk: Technical/enterprise feel might not land emotionally
- Supporting text: "The open-source proxy for visibility and policy enforcement on AI coding agents"

**5. Two env vars. Total visibility.**
- Formula: Concrete + Outcome
- Why it works: Extremely specific, memorable differentiator
- Risk: Only lands with people who know what env vars are (fine for target audience)
- Supporting text: "Point your AI coding agent at Luthien and see everything it does"

**6. Ship AI code confidently.**
- Formula: Action + Emotion
- Why it works: "Confidently" is the resolution (vs. anxiety)
- Risk: Doesn't specify HOW you get confident
- Supporting text: "Real-time visibility and policy enforcement for AI coding agents"

### Tier 3: Experimental

**7. The proxy between you and your AI.**
- Formula: Infrastructure positioning
- Why it works: Clear technical positioning
- Risk: "Proxy" is jargon; might not convey value

**8. AI coding, supervised.**
- Formula: Capability + qualifier
- Why it works: One-word twist adds meaning
- Risk: "Supervised" might sound controlling/corporate

**9. Trust but verify. For AI code.**
- Formula: Familiar phrase + application
- Why it works: Echoes Reagan, feels wise
- Risk: Dated reference, might feel paranoid

## Final Recommendation: Top 3

### #1: "Let AI code. Stay in control." (99% confidence)

**Why this is the winner:**
- Resolves the core tension developers feel (AI doing work vs. losing oversight)
- Parallel structure is memorable ("Let... Stay...")
- "Stay in control" implies you already have control, not that you're gaining it
- Works for both individual devs and teams
- Doesn't sound paranoid or restrictive—sounds empowering

**Best supporting text:**
"The drop-in proxy that gives you visibility and policy enforcement for AI coding agents. Two env vars to see everything."

---

### #2: "AI coding with guardrails." (95% confidence)

**Why this is strong:**
- "Guardrails" is a concrete, positive metaphor (safety, not restriction)
- Very short and memorable (4 words)
- Implies you can still go fast—just with protection
- Appeals to both safety-conscious devs and teams needing compliance

**Best supporting text:**
"Real-time visibility and rule enforcement for Claude Code, Codex, and any AI coding agent. Drop-in proxy. Open source."

---

### #3: "Two env vars. Total visibility." (90% confidence)

**Why this is compelling:**
- Extremely concrete (technical specificity builds trust)
- Memorable contrast (tiny input → big outcome)
- Differentiates from competitors who require code changes
- Speaks directly to developers (they know what env vars are)

**Best supporting text:**
"Point your AI coding agent at Luthien and see every request, every response, in real-time. Enforce rules without changing your code."

## Alternative for Testing

If "control" tests negatively in user research, consider:
- **"AI coding you can trust."** (5 words, trust-focused)
- **"Ship AI code. Sleep easy."** (5 words, outcome-focused)

---

## Final Notes

The winner ("Let AI code. Stay in control.") works because:
1. It's about **permission** (you're letting AI do something) not restriction
2. It resolves the core **emotional tension** developers feel
3. It's **memorable** due to parallel structure
4. It **differentiates** from general LLM tools by specifying "AI code"
5. It **scales** from individual dev to enterprise without changing

Confidence level: 99% that one of these three is the right direction. Would recommend A/B testing #1 vs #2 with actual users if time permits.
